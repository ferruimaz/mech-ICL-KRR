Interpretation
--------------
The revised Experiment 2 says that the dx40 checkpoint does contain a prediction-risk-rank KRR
source subspace under the target-rich diagnostic. In the response-only span, the transition happens
between layers 1 and 2. This is very close to the RBF pattern, but with a lower mean r_T and with
the caveat that dx40 has rare catastrophic model-response failures.

The important separation is:
- subspace availability succeeds by layer 2 on ordinary episodes;
- model use is imperfect, with non-catastrophic E(F,T) around 0.13;
- catastrophic episodes are not explained by missing subspace availability, since the layerwise
  spans become full or near-full while the model map itself fails.

So dx40 is not mainly a missing-dimension result under the prediction-risk-rank definition. It is a
use/alignment/robustness failure: the relevant KRR subspace is available, but the checkpoint does
not reliably implement the target-rich operator across the full dx40 spectrum-capped regime.
