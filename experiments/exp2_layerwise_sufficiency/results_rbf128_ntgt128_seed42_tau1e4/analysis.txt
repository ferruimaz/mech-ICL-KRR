RBF 128x128 layerwise sufficiency analysis

Primary run:
  checkpoint: experiments/rbf_elbow_training/results/nctx128_ntgt128_scratch_seed42/checkpoint_final.pt
  n_ctx=128, n_tgt=128, episodes=8
  candidate threshold tau_sv=1e-4
  risk tolerance alpha=0.05

Direct model quality:
  mean E(F,T)=0.03058
  mean model excess over KRR risk=0.02033
  mean prediction-risk rank r_T=24.75
  mean strict rank=47.13

Layerwise containment:
  Response basis reaches the 5% KRR-risk criterion reliably by layer 2.
  At layer 2: candidate dim=84, rank_alpha=26.9, excess at r_T=0.058.
  By layer 3-6: rank_alpha is about 25-26 and excess at r_T is about 0.05.
  By layers 7-8: excess at r_T is 0.048, essentially matching the oracle risk-rank criterion.

  Raw basis does not reach the 5% criterion until the final block.
  Layers 1-6 have useful but insufficient raw spans.
  Layers 7-8 reach alpha with rank about 26 and excess at r_T about 0.053-0.054.

  Combined basis behaves like response basis and reaches alpha by layer 2.

Interpretation:
  The KRR-relevant source subspace appears early in the label-response geometry
  and becomes clean by late layers. The raw hidden representation only becomes a
  sufficient containment certificate near the end. This supports the idea that
  Experiment 2 should track layerwise response-subspace sufficiency rather than
  individual causal damage.

Threshold note:
  tau_sv=1e-3 is too conservative and excludes weak hidden singular directions
  that are prediction-relevant. tau_sv=1e-8 is too permissive and effectively
  gives a full source span, making the diagnostic tautological. tau_sv=1e-4 is
  the first useful middle setting from this quick sweep.
