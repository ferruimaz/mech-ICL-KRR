Small linear dx layerwise sufficiency analysis

Run:
  checkpoints: dx3, dx5, dx8, dx10, dx15
  n_ctx=47, n_tgt=16, episodes=16
  kernel=linear, tau_sv=1e-4, alpha=0.05

Summary:
  In the small linear checkpoints, the prediction-risk rank equals the input
  dimension up to the n_tgt cap: dx3 -> 3, dx5 -> 5, dx8 -> 8, dx10 -> 10,
  dx15 -> 14.94. The layerwise sufficiency diagnostic finds a KRR-sufficient
  source subspace essentially from the embedding/early layers. This is expected
  for the easy linear setting: the KRR-relevant source directions are simple
  low-dimensional functions of the fixed geometry and the labels are injected
  directly at context positions.

Interpretation:
  The new Experiment 2 does not reproduce the old "dimension ratio" story for
  these simple linear checkpoints because it asks a stronger and more direct
  containment question. For small linear tasks, containment is already easy:
  a rank-r_T KRR subspace is available immediately. The nontrivial layerwise
  emergence result is therefore better illustrated by the RBF checkpoint, where
  the response basis transitions from insufficient at layer 0/1 to sufficient
  by layers 2-4.
